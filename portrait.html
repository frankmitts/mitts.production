FIGHT FOR THE LIGHT

“Go kill yourself.” A joke to some, a trigger to me. Validation that nobody understood the pain I was going through and didn’t care enough to try.
That line was a phrase muttered to me and around me repeatedly when I went back to school after losing my dad when I was 10 to depression. He had battled for a long time and felt he couldn’t any longer, taking his own life. All of a sudden, my favourite star in the night sky burnt out and defeated- was gone from my life forever. Let me tell you, there was and never will be anything funny about that.
Day by day, joke by joke, daddy’s little girl was headed down the same path as he. At the young age of 10, I wasn’t thinking about my future. At the young age of 10, all I was thinking about were the ways I could end it.
My biggest message to all of you is to be kind or simply be quiet. We know sticks and stones break our bones but words pierce and stay engraved in the mind and heart longer than most physical wounds. At the end of the day, we are ALL human, wanting nothing more than to be loved and accepted for exactly who we are. Hand in hand, heart to heart, we’re stronger when we aren’t ripping each other apart.”

– Victoria Lavigne, founder of Fight For The Light
